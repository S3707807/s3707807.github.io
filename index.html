Personal Information

brief on who I am as a person

August 1, 2018 Russel Nawinna

I am an Australian born Sri Lankan so naturally I am an avid cricket fan and love a good curry. I speak Sinhalese at home and have previously studied at Monash University, I was studying a Science degree down there but couldn’t really find a spark in myself to keep on trudging along with it, I couldn’t see myself in the field in the next ten years, I was after a bit more diversity and change and Information Technology really was standing out as I found myself intrigued by my friends working on their assignments. The coding aspects and the all round skills they were doing on tasks, ranging from coding to management to implementation and just seemed like a field with a lot of opportunities and so naturally I figured there is a higher chance that I will be able to find something that I love doing hence this assignment is taking me towards realising that a little bit at least, so I enrolled in a couple IT units where I learnt the basics of Java, HTML and few other language and haven’t looked back since. I saw RMIT as an opportunity to follow that dream and set myself on my way. I feel goals are not as important as the process, I mean you may get to do something you absolutely love but if it was ten years of painstaking effort, I’d rather not, I’d rather the process be as enjoyable as the end product but of course that end product will be that much sweeter.

Interest in IT

What intrigues me about the world of IT?

August 1, 2018 Russel Nawinna

My interest in IT was sparked by the world around me, as it is increasingly a digital world in which we inhabit, IT has made the world a more visceral place through a screen from live shows, to games, to movies, music and connecting with people and things in a way never imagined before. One of my close friends, Richard, really sparked the flame though, he is three years into his IT degree already and was constantly on my back and always showing me things that were literally changing the world and how he is becoming part of it as well through graduate programs that are offered in universities. Through his advice and display of the IT world I chose to do a couple units on coding which is more behind the scenes because if you’re not in the backend of the whole set up you don’t see anything but the user interfaces, you may have a general idea of where things may be going in terms of advancement, but don’t exactly know how fast we are moving along. After doing this Java unit I got an idea of how broad IT is as an industry, from coding to design to management to security to projects and the vast fields that IT has to offer in the coming years as even more jobs are going to be opening with the Artificial Intelligence movement.

I chose to come to RMIT as I believe that RMIT delivers a well-rounded Bachelor of IT which covers a lot of areas which you can hone in on and develop when you enter the professional field. RMIT offers micro credentials, graduate programs, structured pathways to learning many areas of the IT field, even my first year doing this assignment I’ve already earmarked a position as a very likely route for me to go down, Project Management sounds very intriguing. During my studies at RMIT I really do hope to go into the working field, more equipped to make a difference and really push the boundaries, whether it be through channels such as Block Chain, the Internet of Thing or Artificial Intelligence, I want to have a solid base of knowledge and skills so by the time I am done by degree, I have a more mapped out idea of exactly where I want to but you never know what the future holds.
